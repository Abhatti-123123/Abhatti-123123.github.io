Finite-Sample Long-Memory Estimation via Detectability Boundaries

This paper develops a finite-sample framework for measuring long-memory structure in equity log-prices using detectability boundaries. The central object is dLTM: the largest fractional differencing order at which hyperbolic ACF tail structure remains statistically distinguishable from noise.

The paper benchmarks the estimator on synthetic ARFIMA data and applies it to a large cross-section of S&P 500 constituents. The broader motivation is to measure the point where long-range dependence ceases to be empirically resolvable, rather than imposing a fixed differencing convention across assets and time.
