Detectability vs Stationarity

Stationarity and detectability are often treated as if they are the same issue. They are not. A series can be technically stationary while still carrying long-range dependence that matters for forecasting. Conversely, a diagnostic can fail to detect persistence simply because the relevant signal is below the resolution of the sample.

This distinction motivates my use of a detectability boundary. Instead of asking only whether a series is stationary, I ask where hyperbolic ACF tail structure stops being empirically resolvable. That boundary is a practical object: it depends on sample size, lag structure, and the geometry of the observed process.

The research question is therefore not just "is the series stationary?" but "how much memory can we actually see, and how much should we remove before modeling?"
