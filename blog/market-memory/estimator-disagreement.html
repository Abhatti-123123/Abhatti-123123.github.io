Why Long-Memory Estimators Disagree

Draft essay. Revise in your voice before sharing publicly.

Long-memory estimators often disagree because they are not all measuring the same thing under real market conditions. Classical frequency-domain methods work well under clean model assumptions, but financial data contain regime shifts, volatility clustering, structural breaks, and microstructure effects.

A frequency-domain estimator may target an asymptotic fractional integration parameter. A tail-geometry method may target where memory remains visible in a finite sample. A forecasting pipeline may care about a third object: the transform that preserves useful signal while removing unstable dependence.

The disagreement is not always a bug. Sometimes it is evidence that the data-generating process is richer than the model each estimator assumes.
