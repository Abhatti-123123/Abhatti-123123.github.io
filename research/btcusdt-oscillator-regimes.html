Intraday Mean Reversion and Oscillator Regimes in BTCUSDT

A 1-minute BTCUSDT study of return EMA features, oscillator construction, correlation stability, regime shifts, and Markov amplitude dynamics.

Question

This project studies whether short-horizon BTCUSDT returns contain stable, exploitable mean-reversion structure. The focus is not a full trading system, but the behavior of intraday features: EMA-smoothed returns, MACD-like oscillators, and the stability of their predictive correlations over time.

Data and features

Let rt denote the 1-minute log return of BTCUSDT. I construct exponential moving averages of returns with halflives 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, and 1000 minutes. Future returns are measured over 1, 5, and 15-minute horizons.

Because the feature and target distributions are heavily non-Gaussian, I rely primarily on rank-based dependence measures such as Spearman correlation rather than Pearson correlation.

Distributional diagnostics

As the EMA halflife increases, the center of the distribution becomes more Gaussian, but substantial tail deviations remain even for very long smoothing windows.

  • EMA features and future returns have strong heavy tails and asymmetric behavior.
  • Jarque-Bera tests reject normality across EMAs and forward returns.
  • Winsorization reduces kurtosis but does not remove the structural non-normality.
  • Longer halflife EMAs become smoother and more Gaussian in the center, but tail deviations remain.

EMA correlation structure

Nearby halflives are highly correlated because they are smoothed versions of the same return process. Correlation decreases as the halflife gap widens: short EMAs respond to microstructure noise, while long EMAs track slower intraday drift.

Correlations between EMA features and future returns are small but systematically negative, indicating weak short-term mean reversion. The effect is strongest at 5 to 15-minute horizons rather than at 1 minute.

Daily stability

EMA10 has one of the strongest negative correlations with 5-minute forward returns. Computing this correlation day by day in 2023 gives an average daily Spearman correlation of approximately -0.0815, with standard deviation around 0.054. The signal is weak, but it is negative on most days and reasonably stable across the year.

Oscillator construction

I construct MACD-style oscillators of the form: 

Osc(t; λ1, λ2, A) = EMA_λ1(t) - A · EMA_λ2(t)

where λ1 and λ2 are selected from the EMA halflife grid and A is selected from {0.5, 1, 2}. These oscillators compare a shorter-term return state against a longer-term trend component.

Main oscillator findings

  • All oscillator correlations with future returns are negative, consistent with mean reversion.
  • Medium/long halflife combinations dominate pure short-term combinations.
  • In 2023, EMA30 versus EMA100 performs well for 5-minute returns, with correlation around -0.06.
  • EMA100 versus EMA300 performs well for 15-minute returns, reaching roughly -0.07.
  • Correlation strength increases with horizon: 1-minute is weakest, 5-minute stronger, and 15-minute strongest.

2023 vs 2024 regime shift

Repeating the analysis on 2024 data gives the same qualitative ranking, but with weaker magnitudes. A 30/100 oscillator that reached about -0.062 correlation with 5-minute returns in 2023 fell to about -0.036 in 2024. A 100/300 oscillator that reached about -0.071 with 15-minute returns in 2023 fell to about -0.044 in 2024.

This suggests the structure did not disappear, but the strength of short-horizon BTCUSDT mean reversion weakened materially between 2023 and 2024.

Sliding-window parameter search

To study whether the best oscillator parameters evolve over time, I run a 1–30 day sliding-window search. For each day and each trailing window length, I compute Spearman correlation between each candidate oscillator and 5-minute forward returns, then record the best parameter set.

The best parameters do not flip randomly. Instead, they show a low-dimensional regime structure:

  • The slow leg λ2 = 1000 dominates persistently.
  • The fast leg λ1 typically stays in the 10 to 30 range.
  • The amplitude A concentrates around 0.5 and 2.
  • The optimal window length often saturates at 30 days.

Markov amplitude regimes

Treating the amplitude A as a discrete state gives highly persistent transition matrices. Pooled across windows, the diagonal transition probabilities are high, especially for A = 0.5 and A = 2. The A = 1 state behaves more like a transient middle state.

For the dominant EMA pairs, the Markov structure is even clearer: low-amplitude and high-amplitude states are persistent, while the intermediate state exits more quickly. This supports the interpretation that mean-reversion strength itself is regime-dependent.

Interpretation

The evidence points to weak but persistent intraday mean reversion in BTCUSDT. The best features are not arbitrary: they tend to compare medium-horizon return pressure against very slow background drift. The feature family is stable, but signal strength changes across regimes.

Further research

  • Compute stationary distributions and mean sojourn times for amplitude regimes.
  • Optimize continuous oscillator weights instead of using discrete A values.
  • Check whether amplitude transitions align with volatility, liquidity, funding, or market-event regimes.
  • Compare BTCUSDT results to other large crypto pairs and FX futures.
  • Fit Hidden Markov Models directly on oscillator and forward-return dynamics.
