Returns Are a Convention, Not a Measurement

Returns are one of the most natural objects in finance. They are economically meaningful, additive over portfolios in useful ways, and central to risk and performance measurement.

But returns are also a transformation: the first difference of the price path. Treating returns as the default empirical object is equivalent to imposing a particular differencing choice on every asset at every time.

My work asks what happens when that choice does not match the operative memory structure of the market. If a smaller differencing order already removes the relevant memory, then further differencing is not revealing innovations. It may be transforming away structure.
