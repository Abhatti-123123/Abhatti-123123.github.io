The Strange Asymmetry of Over-Differencing

Draft essay. Revise in your voice before sharing publicly.

Under-differencing and over-differencing are not symmetric errors.

If we difference too little, residual memory usually announces itself. Autocorrelation remains visible, diagnostics complain, and the modeler knows that dependence has not been fully removed.

If we difference too much, the error is more subtle. We may create artificial anti-persistent structure while making the series appear cleaner. The transformed object can look statistically acceptable while having lost part of the structured component that made forecasting possible.

This is the asymmetry that motivates my broader work: too little differencing is often visible, while too much differencing can hide behind the convention itself.
