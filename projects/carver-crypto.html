Risk-Scaled Crypto Trend Portfolio

A Carver-style multi-asset crypto trend strategy using volatility scaling, covariance adjustment, trend overlays, and transaction-cost-aware portfolio backtesting.

Objective

This project extends Carver-style risk management ideas to a basket of liquid cryptocurrencies. The focus is not simply on finding a trend signal, but on building a controlled portfolio process: scale positions by volatility, account for cross-asset covariance, include transaction costs, and monitor whether drawdowns remain within the intended risk envelope.

Strategy design

  • Trade 1: risk-scaled buy-and-hold baseline with constant annualized volatility targeting.
  • Trade 2: variable risk-scaled buy-and-hold where the risk target changes with fast and slow EWMA volatility.
  • Trade 3: portfolio risk-scaled crypto trend strategy across liquid crypto assets.

Trade 3 is the main portfolio implementation. Positions are first scaled by per-asset volatility, then rescaled using a blended short/long lookback covariance matrix to hit a fixed portfolio volatility target. A trend overlay concentrates exposure in assets with positive trend signals.

Transaction-cost and Sharpe convention

Reported results include a transaction cost of 5 basis points per executed trade. This is important because trend-following systems can look artificially strong if turnover is ignored. I report Sharpe using daily returns and a 0% risk-free rate convention, consistent with collateralized futures-style trading where posted margin is assumed to earn the prevailing risk-free rate.

Interpretation

The realised outcomes fall inside the original design envelope: roughly 20–30% annualized volatility, Sharpe around 0.8–1.2, maximum drawdown below about 25%, and no extreme blow-up in the return distribution. The realised volatility of 19.1% is slightly below the nominal target, suggesting that the covariance and risk-scaling logic was conservative rather than aggressive.

A maximum drawdown of -18.5% is inside the expected downside bound. The Sortino ratio of 1.59 and tail ratio above 1 suggest that downside tails did not dominate P&L, even though returns remain fat-tailed as expected for crypto markets.

Risk limits and liquidation behavior

No portfolio-level risk limits were breached during the backtest. Daily losses occasionally approached the 4–5% range on extreme days, but they did not cluster in a way that suggested regime breakdown. There was no need for forced portfolio liquidation; position sizing remained algorithmic and driven by volatility and trend.

Extensions

  • Regime-based risk overlays using BTC dominance, funding rates, stablecoin flows, or implied volatility.
  • Event-aware exposure throttling around macro announcements, ETF decisions, or exchange-related headlines.
  • Cross-sectional trend tilts toward coins with stronger and more persistent trend forecasts.
  • Small tail-hedge overlay using options or options proxies.
